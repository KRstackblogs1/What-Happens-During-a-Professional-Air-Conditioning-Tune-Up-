What a Professional Air Conditioning Tune Up Includes

A professional air conditioning tune up is a thorough service designed to keep cooling systems running efficiently and reliably. It involves a detailed inspection and routine maintenance performed by licensed technicians to help prevent breakdowns and improve performance.


Typical tune up services include:

  • Thermostat Check: Ensuring the thermostat is correctly set and communicating with the system for consistent temperature control. This can include reviewing program schedules and verifying temperature accuracy.

  • Electrical System Inspection: Examining key electrical components such as wiring, capacitors, and relays to ensure safe and stable operation.

  • Filter Assessment: Checking and replacing air filters to maintain proper airflow and reduce strain on the system.

  • Coil Cleaning: Inspecting and cleaning evaporator and condenser coils to support effective heat exchange and efficient cooling.

  • Refrigerant Evaluation: Testing refrigerant levels and checking for leaks to maintain optimal cooling performance and comply with current regulations.

  • Blower and Fan Examination: Assessing the indoor blower and outdoor fan for proper operation, which is critical for airflow and system efficiency.

  • Drainage System Check: Inspecting and clearing condensate drain lines to prevent clogs, water damage, or mold growth.

  • System Performance Test: Starting up the system to verify steady cooling, balanced airflow, and proper cycling.


These routine tune ups help maintain energy-efficient operation, extend the lifespan of the air conditioning system, and promote reliable comfort throughout the cooling season.


Additional Services Often Included in Routine Maintenance

Routine maintenance often includes helpful services designed to keep cooling systems running smoothly and efficiently while enhancing homeowner comfort and savings.


  • Smart Thermostat Review: Many modern systems feature Wi-Fi-enabled thermostats. Ensuring these devices are properly connected and functioning supports precise temperature control and efficient energy use.

  • Indoor Air Quality Considerations: With air quality increasingly important, especially in areas affected by wildfire smoke, technicians may assess duct cleanliness, humidity levels, and recommend high-quality filters to promote healthier indoor air.

  • Energy Efficiency Suggestions: Technicians provide practical guidance on easy upgrades such as insulating refrigerant lines, using high-efficiency filters, and adopting seasonal cooling practices that help reduce energy use and extend system life.

These proactive services enhance comfort, support energy savings, and promote the long-term health of cooling systems, providing homeowners with reliable, efficient performance.

Typical Tune-Up Duration

Routine maintenance services generally take between 45 and 90 minutes, depending on the age and condition of the cooling system. Older systems or those with airflow restrictions may require additional time to ensure a comprehensive inspection and thorough servicing.


Licensed technicians conduct detailed checks and maintenance during this time to help improve system performance, enhance energy efficiency, and prevent unexpected breakdowns. This careful attention ensures reliable cooling when it’s needed most.

Scheduling regular tune-ups allows homeowners to maintain comfort and system health with minimal disruption.


How Often Should Maintenance Be Scheduled?

Industry standards recommend scheduling professional maintenance at least once a year, ideally in early spring before the cooling season begins. This timing helps ensure systems are ready for optimal performance during the hottest months.


Homes with pets, heavy cooling demands, or a history of repairs may benefit from more frequent checkups to maintain comfort and system reliability.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is included in a tune up?
A tune up typically includes thermostat calibration, electrical system checks, coil cleaning, refrigerant testing, drainage inspection, airflow evaluation, and a full system operation test to ensure everything runs smoothly.


2. Are tune ups worth it?
Absolutely. Regular tune ups help reduce energy waste, prevent costly breakdowns, and extend the lifespan of the cooling system, keeping homes comfortable and efficient.


3. How long does a tune up take?
Most tune ups take between 45 and 90 minutes, depending on the system’s age, size, and overall condition.


4. How often should a tune up be scheduled?
The standard recommendation is once per year. Homes with pets or heavy cooling use may benefit from more frequent seasonal checkups.


5. Can a tune up help lower energy bills?
Yes. Maintaining clean coils, correct refrigerant levels, and proper airflow improves system efficiency, which can lead to noticeable energy savings.
